☕ Cappuccino vs Latte – What’s the Difference?

If you’ve ever stood in line at a coffee shop wondering whether to order a cappuccino or a latte, you’re not alone. These two popular drinks may look similar, but they offer very different experiences.

☕ What is a Cappuccino?

Cappuccino is made with equal parts of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. This balance creates a rich and bold coffee flavor with a thick, airy texture.

It’s perfect for those who enjoy a stronger coffee taste with a creamy foam finish.

☕ What is a Latte?

Latte (also called Caffè Latte) contains espresso and a larger amount of steamed milk, topped with a light layer of foam.

This makes it smoother, creamier, and milder in flavor—ideal for beginners or anyone who prefers a softer coffee taste.

🔍 Key Differences

  • Cappuccino: Strong, bold, more foam
  • Latte: Smooth, mild, more milk
💡 Barista’s Tip: If you’re new to coffee, start with a latte. As your taste develops, try a cappuccino for a richer experience.
